How to Interpret a Team's Season

Compare a Team to the Rest of the League

[Power Ranking] A team's power and rank tells you how a team compares to every team. They also allow you to evaluate how close two teams are. [Power Ranking] Wins vs Top 25, Top 50, etc. show how a team stacked up against their competition during the season. [Conferences] A team's ranking within their conference tells you how they compare to the rest of the conference in terms of quality.

Compare a Team's Performance throughout the Season

[Ranking Trend] A team's trend allows you to see the story of the season and how they have proven themselves throughout the season.

Compare a Team's Dominance / their Opponents Dominance

[Season Breakdown] Game Dominance adjusts a team's winning percentage for the margin of the game. This breaksdown how "dominant" a team has been regardlesss of whether it was higher or low scoring game. This answers if a team is winning more than the margin of games implies.

Compare a Team's Expected Win Percentage to their Actual Win Percentage

[Season Breakdown] Run Utilization estimates a team winning percentage based on total runs earned and allowed. A team should win in proportion to their "quality" - a ratio of runs earned and runs allowed. If a team's win percentage exceeds their run utilization, they have utilized runs more efficiently.
