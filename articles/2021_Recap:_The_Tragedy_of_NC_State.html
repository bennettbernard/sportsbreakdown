2021 Recap: The Tragedy of North Carolina State

They carry themselves like a team that was designed for this moment, all playing for the university they love. In other words, they were built for this. Just like their coach.

-ESPN.com "College World Series 2021 - Elliot Avent and NC State were built for this moment"

That article was published on June 25, 2021.

On June 26th, the Washington Post ran a headline "N.C. State, derailed by covid-19 issues, is out of the College World Series after NCAA decision"

College baseball fans watched the events playout in real time. In 48 hours the hottest team in baseball was disqualified from the Colelge World Series, despite only 1 loss.

They began the season 4-6 and were ranked 94th in the initial power ranking. They'd be swept by Louisville, turn around and sweep North Carolina, then lose 2 of 3 vs Clemson. Momentum began to build for then #52 NC State. They stole a victory over #12 Notre Dame and swept #35 Virginia Tech. In mid-May, they'd sweep #26 Pitt and take 2 of 3 vs #16 Florida State.

#20 NC State would get a regional pairing against #29 Louisiana Tech. They'd beat Alabama and Louisiana Tech twice to go to the Super Regionals against #1 Arkansas. Arkansas would take game 1 in domiant fashion. But NC State would come back with two 1-run upsets over the #1 team in the nation, and punch their ticket to Omaha.

After defeating #7 Stanford, #9 NC State would take on #3 Vanderbild, a pitching duel decided by a Terrell Tatum home run in the 5th.

NC State's roster would be depleted for the following game due to COVID testing and cases, resulting in a 1-3 loss. The rest is a tragedy of the COVID-19 era. NC State would be disqualified from their third game against Vanderbilt, ending their national title run.

NC State would finish their season power ranked #10, having played the 7th most difficult schedule in the nation.
